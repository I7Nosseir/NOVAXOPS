Your workspace — every page and what it does

Role tags: Everyone  ·  Managers & senior roles  ·  Admin only

Open the platform at perfumeexhibition.com. The sidebar on the left is your navigation. Each item takes you to a different page. You only see the pages your role has access to — if something is missing, contact the admin.

Dashboard Everyone

Your daily overview. Live KPIs across all clients: reach, engagement, active tasks, scheduled posts. Weekly activity chart and a feed of the latest published content with real performance numbers.

Refresh — pulls latest social numbers from the scheduling platform.
Pipeline Everyone

The main task board. 10 stages from Strategy to Reporting. Every piece of work lives here as a card. Drag cards between stages. Click any card to open the full task detail and AI agents.

New Task — create a task. Filter chips — filter by client, assignee, or priority.
My Tasks Everyone

Shows only the tasks assigned to you — across all clients. Your personal to-do list. Check this at the start of each day. Click any task to open the detail panel.

Filter — by stage, priority, or client. Search — find a specific task by name.
Clients Everyone

All client brands in one list. Click a client to see their brand identity, tone of voice, active tasks, and the AI memory built up over time. Crisis Mode toggle flags sensitive situations across the whole platform.

New Client — 9-step onboarding wizard. Add Entry — save a new insight to the client's AI memory.
Projects Managers

Campaign containers. Tasks belong to projects. See progress bars, stage distribution, and timeline at a glance. Filter between active and completed campaigns.

New Project — create a campaign and assign it to a client.
Publishing Everyone

The content calendar and scheduling tool. Write a post, pick platforms and a time, and it goes live automatically. Switch between Grid and Calendar view. Import a spreadsheet to bulk-schedule a full month.

Compose — write and schedule a post. Generate Calendar — AI drafts a month of post ideas from a brief.
Approval Managers

Client sign-off portal. Create an approval request, add the posts, and send the client a branded link. No PDF, no email attachments. The client approves or requests changes per post. You see their decisions live.

New Request — build a batch of posts for client review. Send Email — sends the link to the client automatically.
Moderation Everyone

Your comment and DM inbox. Comments from social platforms arrive here. The AI drafts a reply in the client's brand voice. Review, edit, then send — or escalate to a senior team member.

AI Reply — generates a suggested reply. Escalate — flags for senior review. Ignore — archives spam.
Assets Everyone

The shared media library. Upload files directly or import from Google Drive. Search by name, filter by client or file type. Everything the team needs in one place — no more hunting through Drive folders.

Upload — drag-and-drop files. Import from Drive — browse and pull files from Google Drive.
Reports Managers

Client performance reports generated in one click. Select a client and date range. The platform pulls the data, the AI writes the narrative, and you export a branded PDF or PowerPoint. What used to take hours takes two minutes.

AI Narrative — writes the performance summary. Export PDF / Export PPTX — branded output ready to send.
Workload Managers

Team capacity view. One card per team member showing their task count and a load bar — green means healthy, red means overloaded. See who has room for new work at a glance and redistribute tasks before things pile up.

Click any team member card to see their current task list.
AI Assistant Everyone

A free-form AI chat that knows your clients and tasks. Ask it to draft copy, brainstorm ideas, summarise a brief, or explain a trend. Scope it to a specific client for more relevant answers. Not a generic chatbot — it has your brand context built in.

Type your request. Prefix with a client name to get client-specific answers.
Documents Everyone

In-platform document editor for briefs, SOPs, strategy docs, and meeting notes. Rich text editor with headings, tables, and formatting. Share with a public read-only link. Start from a template or blank.

New Document — blank or from template. Share — generates a read-only link. Export PDF — download.
Creative Eval Managers

Upload a design or video and the AI scores it across 5 dimensions before it goes to the client: brand fit, hook strength, visual quality, CTA clarity, and engagement prediction. Use the score to brief the designer with precision instead of vague feedback.

Upload — drop in an image or video file. Scores appear within seconds.
Performance Managers

Deep analytics per post, competitor tracking, and AI pattern detection. See which formats are winning, when your audience is most active, and how competitors are growing. Data synced daily from the scheduling platform.

Sync — pulls latest post stats. Add Competitor — start tracking a competitor account.
Settings Admin only

Platform configuration. Integrations (API connections), Team management (invite users, set roles), and page permissions (control what each person can see). Everything that keeps the platform running.

Invite User — send an onboarding email to a new team member with their role and access settings.

How work moves — the six workflows you will use every day

Follow these end-to-end. Everything in the platform connects through these flows.

1
Brief to Published Post
Whole team
  1. Receive a brief. Go to Pipeline → New Task. Set client, title, brief text, stage = Strategy, assign to strategist.
  2. Strategist opens the task → runs Task Analyzer to check the brief is complete.
  3. Card moves through Copy → Design → Review by dragging.
  4. Copywriter opens the task → runs Copywriter agent → picks the best variant.
  5. Account manager goes to Approval → creates a request → sends link to client.
  6. Client approves → card moves to Scheduled → social manager opens Publishing → Compose, adds the approved copy and media, sets date and time.
  7. Post publishes automatically. Card moves to Published.
End state: post is live, task is in Reporting stage with performance tracked
2
Client Approval
Account managers
  1. Go to Approval → New Request. Select the client. Add the posts that need sign-off. Set a review deadline.
  2. Click Send Email — the client receives a branded link. No login required on their end.
  3. Client opens the link, reviews each post image + caption, and marks Approve or Request Changes with optional notes.
  4. Their decisions appear live in your Approval panel. If changes requested, the notes show inline — address them and re-send.
End state: signed-off content ready to schedule, no email threads
3
Handling a Comment
Social managers
  1. Open Moderation — new comments appear in the queue, newest first, with the platform and account they came from.
  2. Read the comment. Click AI Reply — the AI drafts a reply in the client's brand voice.
  3. Edit the draft if needed. Click Send.
  4. For complaints or sensitive comments: click Escalate to flag for a senior team member. For spam: Ignore.
End state: comment resolved, archived, and team notified if escalated
4
Monthly Report
Account managers
  1. Go to Reports. Select the client and set the date range (e.g. June 1–30).
  2. The platform pulls performance data automatically from the scheduling platform.
  3. Click AI Narrative — the AI writes a 3-paragraph performance summary in plain, client-ready English.
  4. Review the narrative and make any edits. Click Export PDF or Export PPTX.
  5. Send the file directly to the client.
End state: branded report ready in under 5 minutes
5
Scheduling a Month of Content
Social managers
  1. Go to Publishing → Generate Calendar. Select the client, platforms, and content themes for the month.
  2. The AI drafts a full calendar of post ideas — titles, captions, platform, and suggested dates.
  3. Review and adjust. Delete or rewrite any post that does not fit.
  4. Click Schedule All — all posts queue in the scheduling platform automatically.
  5. Alternatively: upload a pre-prepared Excel sheet via the Import button to bulk-schedule.
End state: full month of content live in the calendar in one session
6
Adding a New Client
Account managers, admin
  1. Go to Clients → New Client. Work through the 9-step wizard: Identity → Social platforms → Competitors → Brand description → Tone of voice → Target audience → Strategy goals → KPI targets → Team resources.
  2. Once saved, the client appears across all pages — pipeline, publishing, reports, everything.
  3. Open the client and go to the Intelligence tab. Add the first context entry: a key win, a brand voice example, or a known audience insight. This is the seed of the client's AI memory.
End state: client fully onboarded, AI knows their brand, team can start work immediately
The pipeline is the single source of truth. If a task is not in the pipeline, it does not exist for the team. Get every brief into the platform from day one — do not keep work in WhatsApp, Notion, or email. The whole system only works when work flows through it.

AI on every task — and what is ready at launch

The AI reads the client's brand identity and context before writing anything. It is not a generic tool — it knows your clients.

The five AI agents — inside every task on the pipeline

Open any task card and go to the AI Agents tab. Each agent is a button. Click it, get a result. The output is cached after the first run — re-running is instant and free. Rate each output with thumbs up or down. The AI learns from your ratings over time and improves per client.

Agent Best used when What you get
Task Analyzer Brief just arrived Flags missing information, identifies the right approach, and breaks the brief into clear execution steps. Run this first on any new task.
Copywriter Task is in Copy stage Writes 3 copy variants for the post: Aspirational, Benefit-led, and Conversational. English or Arabic depending on the client. No hashtags or emojis unless the brief asks for them.
Researcher Before starting ideation Pulls market context, competitor gaps, and trending angles relevant to this client and brief. Gives the copywriter and strategist a running start.
Asset Finder Looking for visuals Reads the brief and produces a list of keywords to search in the asset library and Google Drive. Saves the designer from starting a search from scratch.
Post Caption Visual is ready Attach the final image or video to the task and run this agent. It writes a caption specifically for that visual — not a generic caption from the brief.

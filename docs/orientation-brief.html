The walkthrough — what to show and what to say

Total time: 35–45 minutes. One screen. No slides needed — the app is the presentation.

Open the app before the meeting. Log in as admin. Have a real client open (not Lumara — use an actual client the CEO knows). The goal is to show that this is live, real, and already working — not a prototype.

1
Dashboard
4–5 minutes

Open the dashboard. Don't explain it. Let him look. Then say: "This is every client, live. Reach, engagement, active tasks, posts scheduled — one screen." Point to the weekly activity chart: "This is real data from Metricool, updated every day."

Wow moment: he sees all clients in one view for the first time
2
Pipeline
5–6 minutes

Go to the pipeline. Drag a task from one stage to the next. Then say: "Every piece of work the team touches lives here. Brief to published — 10 stages, drag and drop. No ClickUp. No WhatsApp threads. Every task has an owner, a deadline, a priority." Filter by one client so he sees how it looks per client.

Wow moment: "this is what ClickUp does — and it's already inside the app"
3
Publishing
4–5 minutes

Switch to calendar view. Show a week of scheduled posts. Say: "Everything scheduled for every client, every platform, in one calendar. The team writes the post here, picks the time, and it goes live automatically through Metricool. No copy-pasting. No switching tabs."

Wow moment: the calendar view is immediately readable and clean
4
Approvals
5 minutes

Open the approval page. Show him the shareable link. Open it in a new tab so he sees the client-facing view. Say: "The client gets this link. They see each post with the image and caption. They approve or request changes per post. The team gets an email the second the client submits. No WhatsApp. No PDF. No back and forth."

Wow moment: the client portal looks professional enough to send to a KSA client right now
5
Reports
5–6 minutes

Go to Reports. Pick a real client. Say: "Watch this." Click generate. Let the loading run. When it finishes: "That's a full client report — performance breakdown, narrative analysis, recommendations — exported as a branded PowerPoint or PDF in one click. What used to take 3–5 hours per client."

Wow moment: the strongest demo in the meeting — nothing to explain, it speaks for itself
6
Performance
3–4 minutes

Open Performance for one client. Show the post grid — each post with its engagement numbers. Say: "Every post we've ever scheduled — reach, likes, engagement rate, all tracked automatically. We can also track competitor accounts and see exactly how they're growing."

Wow moment: competitor tracking — most agencies don't have this at all
7
Clients
3 minutes

Open one client profile. Show the brand identity, tone of voice, audience. Say: "Every client has a full brief built into the system. Anyone on the team who touches this client works from the same brief — not a Notion doc, not a PDF, not from memory."

Wow moment: shows discipline and brand consistency across the team
8
Close + tease
2–3 minutes

After showing everything, say: "What I've shown you is the operational layer — task management, publishing, approvals, reports. There's a second layer I haven't shown yet. It handles content creation, strategy, and campaign planning — entirely through AI. I'll show you that separately once we've aligned on this part."

This plants the seed. He will ask about it. That is when you control the next conversation.
If anyone asks "did you use AI to build this?" — Say yes, directly. "The AI handled the repetitive parts — boilerplate code, structure. Every decision about what to build and how it works came from understanding how this agency operates." Do not be defensive. Being direct here builds credibility.

Non-negotiable rules for the deal
IP never transfers You own the code. Always. The agency gets a license to use it — not ownership. If he stops paying, the license ends. Source code never leaves your hands.
Software contract is separate from salary Two different things. Salary = social media specialist role. Retainer = software license and development. Never let them be combined into one number.
Never accept "we'll sort it later" Leave the room with a number agreed or do not leave. A handshake with no figure attached is worth nothing.
Never sell exclusivity for free If he says "I don't want you selling this to others" — that is a premium. Exclusive rights cost more, not the same. Price it higher if he asks for exclusivity.
The studio tools are your second act. Everything shown in this walkthrough is the operational layer — already impressive on its own. The AI generation capabilities (content studio, strategy, campaigns, hooks) are held back deliberately. Once the CEO has seen the operational layer and the commercial conversation is done, the studio reveal becomes a demonstration of what is still coming — and reinforces why the retainer is worth paying long term.
